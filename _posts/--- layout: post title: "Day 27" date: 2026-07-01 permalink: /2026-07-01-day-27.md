what_i_learned: |
   Today, our research team focused on advancing the methodology and dataset development for our DermaBridge project. Each team member was assigned a subsection of the methodology to write collaboratively on Overleaf, and my responsibility was the Model Architectures section. In this section, I described the deep learning models used in our study, including ResNet50, EfficientNet-B0, EfficientNetV2-M, ConvNeXt, and the Swin Transformer, explaining their architectural designs and why they were selected for skin lesion classification. Writing this section strengthened my understanding of the differences between convolutional neural networks and transformer-based models, as well as their respective strengths in medical image analysis. In addition to writing the methodology, I researched new datasets that could enhance the diversity and robustness of our training data. For each dataset, I documented its source, dataset size, skin tone coverage, and access link to help the team evaluate its suitability for our project. Among the datasets I identified were the SD-198 Skin Disease Dataset and the ASAN Dataset, a Korean dermatology dataset that provides representation across diverse skin tones. These activities contributed to improving both the documentation of our research methodology and the quality of the datasets considered for future model development.
