what_i_learned: |
  The first session of the day was another literature review workshop led by Dr. Anita Pandey. During the session, she introduced a structured approach for organizing research articles by tabulating key information such as the author, year of publication, research focus, hypothesis, findings, and limitations. This framework provided a systematic way to analyze and compare sources while building a strong foundation for our literature review.
Following this, we met with our graduate mentor for the research lab session. During this time, my group and I searched for scholarly articles and research papers relevant to our project topic. We then applied the Resource Planning Guide to update our individual resource tables and organized the information we gathered. Afterward, we briefly reviewed several of the papers, discussing their methodologies, key findings, and relevance to our research objectives.
After lunch, we returned to our breakout rooms and continued working in the research lab. A significant portion of this session was dedicated to preparing and refining our project dataset. We expanded abbreviated diagnosis labels, added binary values to indicate malignancy status, and addressed missing data to improve the dataset's quality and usability. These preprocessing steps were essential for ensuring that the data would be accurate, consistent, and ready for future analysis.
We concluded the day by collaborating on our weekly report presentation slides. This gave us the opportunity to summarize the progress we had made throughout the week and organize our next steps for the following week.
