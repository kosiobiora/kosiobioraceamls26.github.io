what_i_learned: |
   Today marked the Mid-Summer Symposium, where my research group presented our project, DermaBridge: A Trustworthy Multimodal AI App for Skin Lesion Screening, Explainable Risk Assessment, and Smart Clinical Referral. Our presentation highlighted the project's objectives, including improving skin lesion classification through multimodal deep learning, reducing algorithmic bias across different skin tones, incorporating explainable AI techniques, and developing a prototype application to support clinical decision-making. During my portion of the presentation, I discussed the two major research gaps we identified through our literature review: algorithmic bias caused by the underrepresentation of darker skin tones in dermatology datasets and the translational gap between laboratory model performance and real-world clinical deployment. I also presented my contributions to the project, including conducting literature reviews, cleaning and standardizing datasets, training multiple deep learning models such as EfficientNetB0, ResNet50, and EfficientNetV2, and co-authoring sections of our research paper. In addition, I explained some of the challenges I encountered throughout the project, particularly learning advanced machine learning concepts, preprocessing complex datasets, and identifying meaningful research gaps, along with the strategies I used to overcome each of them. I concluded by outlining the next phase of the project, which includes integrating our best-performing model into the DermaBridge application, implementing explainability tools such as Grad-CAM and SHAP, developing the full application interface, and conducting comprehensive system testing and fairness evaluations. Following our presentation, faculty members, mentors, and scholars from other research groups asked thoughtful questions and provided constructive feedback that encouraged us to think more critically about our methodology and future development plans.
