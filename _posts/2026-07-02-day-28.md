what_i_learned: |
   Today’s session focused on advancing both the research documentation and dataset analysis for our DermaBridge project. I continued the exploratory data analysis (EDA) process by examining additional skin lesion datasets, documenting key characteristics such as dataset shape, class distribution, Fitzpatrick skin tone coverage, and the presence of missing values. This analysis helped our team evaluate the strengths and limitations of each dataset and determine which ones would contribute to building a more diverse and representative training dataset. In addition to the data analysis, I spent time reviewing our research paper in Overleaf, carefully linking every in-text citation in the Abstract, Introduction, and Literature Review to the corresponding reference in the bibliography. I also standardized the reference formatting using the IEEE citation style and verified that there were no floating numbers or missing references, improving the overall consistency and professionalism of the manuscript. During the day, we attended a one-hour lecture delivered by our faculty mentor, Dr. Saroj K. Pramanik on the relationship between genetics and skin cancer. The lecture explored how genetic mutations, inherited risk factors, and molecular mechanisms influence the development and progression of skin cancer. Learning about the genetic basis of skin cancer deepened my understanding of the disease beyond the computational perspective and reinforced the importance of developing fair and reliable diagnostic tools. By the end of the session, I had contributed to both strengthening the quality of our research paper and improving our understanding of the datasets that will support the next phase of model development.
