what_i_learned: |
  Today marked the first day of the physical portion of the Summer AI Research Institute. In the opening session, we were introduced to the structure of the program over the coming weeks, including participant responsibilities. Following this, we attended a Data Analytics Workshop led by Dr. Jamell Dacon. This session introduced key concepts in data analysis and highlighted how analytical thinking is applied in real-world research contexts. We then participated in a session on AI Bias and Ethics facilitated by Dr. Valerie Riggs, where we explored how to distinguish between random error and systematic bias in AI outputs. The discussion emphasized the limitations of every tool, and the importance of recognizing that these systems make decisions that affect real people. A key takeaway was the question of who builds these systems and how their design choices shape outcomes. Afterward, we took part in a hands-on team challenge where we were tasked with building a tower capable of supporting a marshmallow at the top. I completed this exercise with my group, and it helped strengthen our collaboration, communication, and problem-solving skills under time constraints. The final activity of the day was the research lab session, where my graduate mentor provided an overview of the activities my group and I will be working on in the coming days.
