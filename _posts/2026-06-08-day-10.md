what_i_learned: |
  Today's activities were primarily focused on the research lab sessions. During this, my group and I worked on combining two skin lesion datasets, HAM10000 and PAD
  UFES 20, into a single dataset for future analysis. We started by loading and examining both datasets, then cleaned and standardized the data so that they shared
  the same format. We also handled missing values and created a common set of metadata fields. After cleaning the datasets, we merged them into one metadata file and
  added grouped Fitzpatrick skin tone categories to support future fairness analysis. We then reviewed the distribution of the data to better understand how skin
  tone information was represented in the merged dataset. Next, we combined all image files from both datasets into a single image folder. We verified that the
  images matched the metadata correctly and checked that every record could be linked to its corresponding image. Finally, we prepared the dataset for machine
  learning by testing an image preprocessing function and building a data loader that could retrieve both images and metadata. By the end of the session, we had
  created a unified dataset and confirmed that it was ready for future analysis and model development.
