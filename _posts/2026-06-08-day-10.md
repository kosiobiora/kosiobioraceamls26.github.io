what_i_learned: |
  Today's session began with a Computer Vision lesson led by Daniel Onyejiekwe. During the session, he explained how computer vision enables computers to interpret and understand images by breaking them down into data, identifying patterns, detecting objects, and learning from examples. We also explored some of the real-world applications of computer vision in areas such as healthcare, security, and autonomous systems. To reinforce these concepts, we participated in the Teachable Machine activity, where we trained a simple machine learning model to recognize different household objects. This hands-on exercise helped us better understand how image classification models are created and evaluated. Afterward, we completed the OpenCV and YOLO activities, where we learned how computer vision libraries can process and manipulate images through tasks such as image loading and object detection. This allowed us to see how advanced machine learning models can quickly identify and label multiple objects within a scene.
After lunch, we returned to our breakout rooms and focused on preparing our weekly showcase presentation, which involved reviewing the work we had completed throughout the week. My group and I collaborated on refining our slides, organizing our findings, and ensuring that our progress was communicated clearly and effectively. The day concluded with each group presenting their weekly report to the larger cohort, giving us the opportunity to share our accomplishments, receive feedback, and learn about the progress of other teams.
