what_i_learned: |
  Today's sessions began with the research lab, where my group and I continued refining and revising the dataset we had worked on the previous day. Our graduate mentor then led a detailed walkthrough of the dataset, explaining the purpose of the various functions, commands, and libraries used throughout the data cleaning, preprocessing, and preparation process. This helped us better understand not only what each line of code was doing, but also why those steps were necessary for building a reliable dataset. Afterward, we attended a literature review session led by Dr. Anita Pandey. During the session, she discussed the importance of using effective transition words in research writing and explained how they can improve the flow, clarity, and organization of written work, presentations, and discussions. Later, we returned to our groups and continued the research lab activities. During this, we explored the role of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) in deep learning and learned how they are commonly used for image classification tasks. We also examined several key components of CNNs, including convolutional layers, pooling layers, activation functions, and fully connected layers, and discussed how each contributes to the model's ability to learn from image data. To reinforce our understanding, we completed a quiz based on the concepts covered. We also learned about several data encoding techniques used in machine learning, including One Hot Encoding, Label Encoding, Ordinal Encoding, Binary Encoding, Frequency Encoding, and Target Encoding. The day concluded with a hands on exercise in which we applied these encoding methods, giving us practical experience with preparing categorical data for use in machine learning models.
