what_i_learned: |
  The first activity in today’s session was the research lab, where we were split into breakout rooms and had the opportunity to discuss the progress of our projects with our research groups. During this time, my faculty mentor, Dr. Saroj Pramanik, gave an overview of our project and delivered an in-depth lecture on skin cancer, helping us better understand the impact of the disease. He also explained the relevance of our research and how artificial intelligence can be used to improve the detection and diagnosis of skin cancer. Following this discussion, we were assigned the task of finding and reviewing five academic papers related to our project in order to build a stronger foundation in the subject area. After we reconvened, we continued with the Python 101 lessons, where we worked through several practice problems that reinforced the programming concepts we have learned so far. After lunch, we delved into an Introduction to Generative AI and Large Language Models (LLMs) session, where we learned about the foundations of these technologies and how they are trained on large amounts of data. We were then introduced to several AI tools and agents, including ElevenLabs and Suno, and were encouraged to experiment with them to better understand their capabilities. At the end of each section of this lecture, we participated in Kahoot quizzes that helped reinforce key concepts while keeping the session interactive and engaging. Finally, the day concluded with a weekly feedback discussion, where all participants had the opportunity to reflect on our experiences in the program so far and share suggestions for making the coming weeks even better.
