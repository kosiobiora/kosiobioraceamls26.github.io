what_i_learned: |
  Today's session began in the research lab, where my group and I reviewed our project checklist to better track our progress and ensure that each stage of the work was properly aligned with our goals. We also brainstormed potential features for DermaBridge, the AI-powered app we are building for skin lesion screening, explainable risk assessment, and smart clinical referrals. This helped clarify the direction we want the machine learning model to take and how it could eventually support meaningful dermatology-related decision-making. Following this, we returned to the main session, where all participants took part in a literature review session led by Dr. Pandey. She introduced the LOCK method, which stands for Language, Organization, Content, and Knowledge, as a structured approach to reading and analyzing research papers more effectively. We also explored how AI tools like ChatGPT can be practically used in research workflows, particularly for locating credible studies, comparing findings across multiple papers, and visualizing key insights. This demonstrated how AI can support not only technical development but also the broader research process. The final activity of the day took place back in the research lab, where my group worked on a separate dataset and carried out important preprocessing steps to prepare it for machine learning. This included label encoding to convert diagnosis categories into numerical values, missing value imputation to handle incomplete data, and class balancing to address imbalances between different diagnosis groups.
