what_i_learned: |
  Today's session began in the research lab, where I reviewed our project checklist to better understand our progress and how each task contributes to the overall goals of DermaBridge. I also participated in discussions about potential features for the application, which helped me think more critically about how machine learning models can be integrated into practical healthcare tools for skin lesion screening, explainable risk assessment, and smart clinical referrals. Following this, I attended a literature review session led by Dr. Pandey, where I learned the LOCK method, which stands for Language, Organization, Content, and Knowledge, as a framework for reading and evaluating research papers more effectively. The session also introduced practical ways to use AI tools such as ChatGPT throughout the research process, including identifying relevant studies, comparing findings across multiple papers, and visualizing key insights. This expanded my understanding of how AI can support not only model development but also research and knowledge discovery. Later in the day, I returned to the research lab and worked with a separate dataset, gaining experience with several important preprocessing techniques. These included label encoding to transform categorical diagnoses into numerical values, missing value imputation to address incomplete records, and class balancing to reduce disparities between diagnosis categories. Through these activities, I developed a deeper understanding of the preparation required before data can be used effectively in machine learning workflow.
